Mike Rockwood
Founder

Much of my life has been spent at the intersection between creativity and technology. I grew up tinkering with early table-based website builders and creating bad electronic music. Eventually, I made my way through the Digital Media program at Utah Valley University. The program was brand new at the time, and future prospects were uncertain, but nothing excited me more than getting to explore web development, filmmaking, music production, and 3D modeling.

My professional career began at a small credit union. During my time there, I gained a wealth of experience writing semantic and accessible HTML, building and maintaining CSS, and utilizing JavaScript to create interactive elements. I also developed a strong understanding of web performance optimization and cross-browser compatibility.

Today I work at University of Utah Health as a senior web developer. At various points this role has involved writing code, designing interfaces, solving complex problems, collaborating with teams, and mentoring others. I was a driving force behind the planning and production of the Gloss design system, which has been adopted throughout the organization.

Even after years of working in the web and technology space, I remain optimistic about its potential to unlock creativity and bring people together. I hope to contribute to this in some small way. Let's build cool stuff!

Interests outside of work

Music

I try to spend a little time each day listening to new music. It is a great way to broaden my musical palette, gain inspiration, and appreciate different cultures. In addition, I have been playing guitar for many years. I love the challenge of learning new songs and techniques.

Economics

After graduating during a recession and getting my first (real) job at a bank, I almost feel like I was forced to take a crash course in economics against my will. Nonetheless, learning about economics has provided powerful insights into human behavior and current events.

Chess

My middle school offered a chess class as an elective. Who could pass up the chance to get school credit for playing games? Thus began an on-again, off-again love affair with chess. Even after years of play I continue to find new ways to appreciate this beautiful and complex game.

Urbanism

Frustrations with the built environment have lead to an interest in urbanism. Urbanism helps us understand the complex dynamics between people and their surroundings, and how cities are planned, function, and impact the environment, society, and culture.
